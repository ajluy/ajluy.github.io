These projects were completed through SIBC (Student Investment and Business Consulting), a college consulting club starting as a member and eventually leading projects in the marketing division. The transition into a project lead role was shaped in part by experience outside of consulting: working on technical projects that required coordinating across moving parts, structuring ambiguous problems, and making decisions under uncertainty carried over into how these engagements were approached and run. Across four projects in CPG, healthcare, media, and technology, the work covered consumer research, customer journey analysis, campaign strategy, product redesign, and brand repositioning.

## P&G Swiffer: Multicultural Growth Strategy
**Spring 2025**
 
The engagement focused on understanding how Swiffer could grow sales within Black and Hispanic households, demographics where the brand had historically struggled to win market share. The team's early hypotheses centered on cultural cleaning habits: specifically the preference for traditional methods and strong scents: and the research bore that out. Hispanic consumers were found to favor traditional hand mopping, while both groups prioritized multi-purpose and antibacterial products.
 
From that, the team developed three main recommendations: a new wet multi-surface tool designed to bridge Swiffer's convenience with the deep-cleaning tradition preferred by those households; packaging redesigns that explicitly called out effectiveness claims like "antibacterial" and "removes 99% of dust & dirt"; and sports sponsorships, specifically NCAA March Madness, as a way to build brand awareness with the target audience through media they already consume.
 
As project lead, the role involved setting the initial research direction, dividing the consumer segments across team members, and running the synthesis sessions where those findings were shaped into the product and packaging recommendations presented to the client.

## Ogilvy & Gerber: Gen Z Market Expansion
**Fall 2024**
 
The goal of this engagement was to recommend how Gerber could market products like Puffs and Melts to the 18–25-year-old college demographic: shifting perception away from a brand associated primarily with infants. The approach was built around primary research: a campus-wide survey was deployed, and competitor benchmarking was run alongside it to give the findings context.
 
The survey showed that 38% of students had already purchased Gerber, but brand misperceptions remained strong. The team's recommendations came out of that data: introducing bolder flavor variations aligned with adult snacking trends, leaning into TikTok and campus influencer content as the main distribution channel for brand repositioning, and improving product accessibility through promotions like Amazon Prime Day.
 
As project lead, the role involved structuring the primary research plan, overseeing the survey deployment, keeping the competitor benchmarking on track, and organizing the team's findings into a campaign strategy that tied the data back to concrete distribution and media recommendations.

## Eli Lilly: Verzenio Patient Retention
**Spring 2024**
 
This project looked at retention for Verzenio, an oncology drug where patients frequently discontinue due to side effects like extreme fatigue and diarrhea. The team's hypothesis was that drop-off was tied to lifestyle disruption, and the research focused on breaking down the patient population into specific segments: working professionals, caregivers, low-income patients, and older adults: to understand how those disruptions played out differently across groups.
 
The recommendations that came out of it were focused on three areas: expanding support programs to help patients manage physical side effects, building an online community forum where patients could share experiences and coping strategies, and improving affordability and access through direct delivery and Lilly Savings cards.
 
As project lead, the role involved framing the segmentation approach, assigning team members to specific patient groups, and running the meetings where individual findings were pulled together into a unified set of support and access recommendations.

## Pinterest: Social Commerce Strategy
**Fall 2023**
 
The engagement looked at how Pinterest could position itself as a go-to shopping destination across fashion, home, and technology: categories where the platform already had strong user engagement but hadn't fully converted that into commerce. Early hypotheses focused on Gen Z's use of Pinterest for planning and aesthetic building, and the research explored how that behavior could be extended into purchasing.
 
The team's recommendations centered on three product directions: native in-app checkout to shorten the path from discovery to purchase, a personalized "Shop" section built around the planning behavior already present on the platform, and a general principle of integrating commerce features without disrupting the inspiration-driven experience that made Pinterest useful to begin with.
 
As project lead, the role involved setting the research scope across commerce categories and demographics, keeping weekly check-ins focused, and making sure the product feature recommendations stayed grounded in the user behavior the research had actually identified rather than drifting toward generic platform suggestions.
