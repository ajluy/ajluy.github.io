I have been working on a series of sculptures for my 3D Foundations class. Each project was an attempt to take a complicated idea, like social conflict or personal ambition, and turn it into a sculpture.

By experimenting with materials like clay, paper, and wood, I explored how different textures and shapes can tell a story about the way we live and work.

Project 1: Clay Figures 

This piece uses clay and acrylic paint to look at a common social problem: why do people fight over things when there is enough for everyone? I used small figures and colorful candy to show how irrational human conflict can be.

![Alt text](/assets/images/3d-foundations/clay_sculpture.jpg)

I purposely spread the candy out to create a sense of aimlessness. The figures are fighting, but the "stakes" are just candy, which makes the whole scene feel a bit absurd. By putting the piece on a tall platform in the center of the room, I wanted viewers to walk all the way around it so they could see several different fights happening at the same time.

Project 2: Work Clothes

This project was made entirely out of paper materials like cardboard, tissue paper, and paper maché. It explores the idea of professional identity and how many people feel heavily defined by their career and major.

![Alt text](/assets/images/3d-foundations/paper_sculpture.png)

I did a lot of research on fashion and then changed the proportions of a standard suit to make it look slightly uncomfortable. I used black tape for sharp lines and added gold buttons to make it look official. The goal was to show that a work suit can be a symbol of success, but it can also feel like a skin you can never take off.

Project 3: 11 Seconds

My final project, is a kinetic sculpture made of wood and wire. This is my most personal work because it represents my own constant drive to achieve more.

![Alt text](/assets/images/3d-foundations/wood_sculpture.jpg)

The sculpture has a sharp diagonal shape that looks like it is breaking out of its frame. There is a marble that rolls in a constant loop, representing ambition that keeps moving but never really arrives at a finish line. To make the piece work, the viewer has to manually spin a small elevator. This forces the audience to participate in the cycle, moving from just watching the art to actually experiencing the "restlessness" themselves.
