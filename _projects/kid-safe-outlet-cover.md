Outlet covers are a tiny but frustrating part of home safety. Most of the ones you find in stores are either impossible to take off or so easy to lose that parents often give up on using them. As part of a intro to entruprenurship class my group decided to tackle this problem by creating a version that actually looks good and works better for busy families, where I did the product design and 3D printing as the engineer in the group.  

The Problem with Current Designs

Existing outlet protectors usually have two big flaws. First, they are often difficult to remove, which is annoying for adults. Second, they do not look very good, which makes people less likely to want them in their homes. Our goal was to fix both of these issues by focusing on "usability" and "aesthetics".  

Version 1: The First Attempt

Our first design was an "add-on" cover that fit on top of an existing outlet. We 3D printed it using ABS plastic, which is a hard and somewhat brittle material. It was designed to stay on the wall with double-sided tape, and you could remove the covers by pinching the sides.  

| ![alt1](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover_sketch.png) | ![alt2](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover.png) |

After testing this version and getting feedback, we realized it had some issues. The ABS plastic was hard to work with, and sticking something over an existing outlet felt like unnecessary work for the user. We knew we needed to make the next version look more professional and be easier to install.  

Version 2: Moving Toward Permanence

For Version 2, we changed our approach. Instead of an add-on, we designed a full outlet plate that screws directly into the wall. We also switched the material to PETG plastic. This material is stronger and softer than ABS, which makes it easier to bend the caps without breaking them.  

| ![alt1](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover_2sketch.png) | ![alt2](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover_2CAD.png) |

We also focused on the visual side of the project. We curved the corners of the caps to give them a sleek look and printed them in white to match standard home decor. We even added a space to seal the caps, which helps make the product more water resistant.  

Version 3: Solving the "Lost Cap" Problem

The final major step was testing our design with kids to see how they interacted with it. The feedback we got pointed out one last problem: parents were still worried about losing the caps once they were removed.  

| ![alt1](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover_final.png) | ![alt1](/assets/images/kid-safe-outlet-cover/outlet_cover_done.png) |

To fix this, Version 3 includes a built-in string holder on both the cap and the outlet plate. This way, when you take the cap off, it just hangs there instead of getting lost. This small change makes the product more reliable and gives it a longer lifespan.
